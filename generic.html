My Story

I'm a fourth-year international student at the University of Toronto. When I was 17 years old, I left my home country and came to Canada. In 2015, I studied at Vancouver high school. Before I applied to undergraduate university, I aimed to pursue the Economics program at the University of Toronto. People always ask me the question: Why do you want to study Economics? I'm attracted by a new view of the world offered by Economics. When people learn Economics, they can evaluate everything from an economic perspective. I'm excited to study Economics at my dream school. After taking the first statistic course that applies programming to economic problems, I found an interest in statistics. Therefore, I chose to major in Economics and Statistics. I have done several data analysis projects and have a research experience at U of T. The undergraduate study spurred my interest in exploring more in Economics, so I want to continue studying in this field in graduate study.
